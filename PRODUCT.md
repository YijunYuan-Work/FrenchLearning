# Product

## Register

product

## Users

French Desk is for adult learners who want a personal, low-friction place to collect French vocabulary, phrases, grammar notes, pronunciation reminders, quizzes, and review cards. They use it in short daily study sessions, often when they want progress without feeling drained.

## Product Purpose

The product helps learners capture French notes, organize them by category and tags, review weak material, and complete a daily practice loop. Success means the user understands what to do next, feels encouraged to return, and can study without wrestling with the interface.

## Brand Personality

Light, encouraging, focused. The interface should feel like a calm study desk with a little spark: useful first, warm second, never childish or noisy.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic beige dashboards, sterile admin panels, childish classroom-game styling, heavy dark mode, decorative gradients, emoji-based UI, and repeated identical card grids that make study feel like chores.

## Design Principles

Make the next study action obvious.

Use warmth to reduce fatigue, not to decorate every surface.

Reward progress with clear, small signals instead of spectacle.

Keep language content readable and scannable.

Prefer familiar product controls with a friendly tone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, readable text sizes, color-independent state indicators, and reduced-motion support. The product should remain usable for learners who study in short bursts or with lower attention/energy.
